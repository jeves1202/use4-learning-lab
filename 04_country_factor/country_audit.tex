\section{Purpose and Scope}

Audits the Country factor: the intercept of the USE4 cross-sectional
regression, with unit exposure for every asset, identified by the
cap-weighted industry constraint $\sum_j w_{jt} f_{\text{ind},j,t} = 0$ (USE4
Methodology Notes \S2.1; Empirical Notes \S4). The build delivers the
\textbf{anchor} (the per-date CSR regression universe with normalized
$\sqrt{\text{mcap}}$ weights) and runs the \textbf{validation CSR} ---
country + 55 industries + 12 styles over every usable month-transition ---
the first end-to-end exercise of the full exposure stack.

\smallskip
\textbf{Scope.} This audit covers \texttt{build\_country.ipynb} as executed on
2026-06-12 (the reference run; the build runs \textbf{last}, after every
exposure build): all printed
diagnostics, the validation battery, and both output parquet schemas. All
numerical values are verbatim from the executed notebook. The notebook
generates no figures. Construction rationale at spec level:
\path{04_country_factor/country_spec.ipynb}.

\section{Known Deviations from PDF Spec}

This section mirrors the NOT-IN-PDF master list from
\texttt{country\_construction\_guide.ipynb} (\S6). The four-part validity
arguments live in the pipeline-level register,
\path{docs/deviations_register.tex}.

\begin{longtable}{@{}p{0.5cm}p{3.6cm}p{6.0cm}p{3.8cm}@{}}
\caption{NOT-IN-PDF decisions (mirrors the guide master list)}\\
\toprule
\textbf{\#} & \textbf{Decision} & \textbf{Chosen solution} & \textbf{Revisit when} \\
\midrule
\endhead
1 & Constraint implementation & Exact reparametrization, eliminating the largest-cap-weight industry per date & Never --- algebraically identical to any exact form; numerically safest divisor \\[3pt]
2 & Regression weights & $\sqrt{\text{mcap}}$ (USE4's idio-variance model), normalized per date; shipped as \texttt{w\_reg} & If the CSR module adopts a different idio-variance model \\[3pt]
3 & Return convention & Sum of daily excess log returns over the trading days in $(t, t{+}1]$ (day assigned by its previous trading day); partial months kept & If the CSR module standardizes differently --- reconcile against the reference series \\[3pt]
4 & Factor-dates without coverage & Drop the style column that date, $f$ = null & Never --- dropping the date discards 60+ valid factor returns to avoid one null \\[3pt]
5 & Eliminated industry & argmax cap weight per date & Never --- exact algebra either way \\[3pt]
6 & Validation artifact & \path{csr_validation_returns.parquet} saved as audit evidence, not a model deliverable & When the CSR module ships its own factor returns --- keep for reconciliation, then retire \\[3pt]
7 & No \texttt{score\_source} & Anchor carries none --- exposure is definitional & Never --- would imply an estimator exists \\[3pt]
8 & Unclassifiable stocks & \texttt{UNASSIGNED (fallback ladder)} rows dropped from the CSR sample, gated $\leq 50$ & If the unassigned population grows past the gate \\[3pt]
9 & RF publication lag & Transitions with no excess returns skipped ($\leq 3$, tail only); picked up on the next data refresh & Never --- mixing raw and excess returns in one series is worse than a short tail \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\section{Stage-by-Stage Audit}

%──────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Stage 1 --- Anchor}

\begin{description}[style=nextline]
  \item[PDF SPEC] Every asset has unit exposure to the country factor; WLS weights $\propto \sqrt{\text{mcap}}$.
  \item[Implementation] In-ESTU universe ($\geq$ 1999-01-01) + \texttt{country} $= 1.0$ + per-date-normalized $\sqrt{\text{mcap}}$ weights.
  \item[Observed]\phantom{x}
    \begin{itemize}[nosep, topsep=-\parskip]
      \item \textbf{822{,}918} rows, \textbf{330} dates; duplicates \textbf{0}; max $|\Sigma\, w_{\text{reg}} - 1|$ per date $= 2.22\times10^{-16}$
      \item \textbf{9.5 MB}; schema check \PASS{PASSED}; read-back \PASS{PASSED}
    \end{itemize}
  \item[Status] \PASS{MATCHES SPEC}
\end{description}

%──────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Stage 2 --- Monthly returns}

\begin{description}[style=nextline]
  \item[PDF SPEC] Factor returns estimated from one-month asset returns against exposures known at the month start.
  \item[Implementation] Daily excess log returns summed over $(t, t{+}1]$ (each trading day assigned by its previous trading day, so exposures at $t$ strictly precede every return they explain); benchmark compounded identically.
  \item[Observed]\phantom{x}
    \begin{itemize}[nosep, topsep=-\parskip]
      \item \textbf{1{,}778{,}631} stock-months; benchmark \textbf{329} months (1999-01-29 $\to$ 2026-05-29)
      \item Final window (2026-05-29 $\to$ 2026-06-10) is the short in-progress month
    \end{itemize}
  \item[Status] \PASS{MATCHES SPEC}
\end{description}

%──────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Stage 4 --- Validation CSR}

\begin{description}[style=nextline]
  \item[PDF SPEC] Constrained cross-sectional WLS: country + industries + styles, $\sum_j w_j f_{\text{ind},j} = 0$.
  \item[Implementation] Exact reparametrization per transition (largest-weight industry eliminated); $\sqrt{v}$-scaled \texttt{lstsq}; style columns without coverage dropped per date; \texttt{UNASSIGNED} stocks excluded.
  \item[Observed]\phantom{x}
    \begin{itemize}[nosep, topsep=-\parskip]
      \item \textbf{327} transitions run (2 skipped: the RF-lag tail, 2026-04-30 and 2026-05-29 starts); \textbf{22{,}236} factor-return rows
      \item Dropped factor-dates: \textbf{9} --- gro/beta/nlb $\times$ the first three 1999 dates (annual-data and benchmark warm-ups)
      \item CSR sample sizes: min \textbf{1{,}791}, median \textbf{2{,}565}, max \textbf{3{,}043}; UNASSIGNED drops \textbf{5}
      \item Constraint pivot frequency led by Banks (113), Pharmaceuticals (56), Software--Infrastructure \& Gaming (42)
    \end{itemize}
  \item[Status] \PASS{MATCHES SPEC}
\end{description}
